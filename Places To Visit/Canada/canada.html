CANADA

Edmonton is the capital city of the Canadian province of Alberta. Edmonton is situated on the North Saskatchewan River and is the centre of the Edmonton Metropolitan Region, which is surrounded by Alberta's central region. The city anchors the northern end of what Statistics Canada defines as the "Calgary-Edmonton Corridor", a region spanning between Edmonton and the city of Calgary, Alberta's largest city, which includes the many smaller municipalities between the two.

For me, Edmonton holds a special appeal due to its unique combination of urban attractions and natural beauty. Firstly, I'm intrigued by the city's vibrant cultural scene. Edmonton is home to a variety of festivals and events throughout the year, including the internationally renowned Edmonton Fringe Festival and the Edmonton International Film Festival. These events promise a rich tapestry of arts and entertainment experiences that I'm eager to explore. Additionally, Edmonton boasts a thriving culinary scene with diverse dining options to suit every palate. From cozy cafes to upscale restaurants, there's a culinary adventure waiting around every corner. I'm especially excited to try local specialties like Alberta beef and traditional Canadian comfort foods.
